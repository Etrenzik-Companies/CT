## Indiana Incentive, Tax, and ESG Stack

### Federal pathways relevant to Indiana execution

- `IRS 179D`: potential deduction if the hotel design achieves qualifying efficiency performance with third-party certification.
- `IRS 48E`: potential credit if the project adds qualifying solar, storage, or other clean-electric scope.
- `DOE Better Buildings`: technical-assistance track for benchmarking and energy planning.

### Indiana and local pathways to diligence

- `Indiana Hoosier Business Investment Tax Credit`: possible state tax-credit support if the investment and job narrative meet IEDC criteria.
- `Indiana Skills Enhancement Fund`: possible support for pre-opening training, workforce ramp, and hotel operations training.
- `Carmel / Hamilton County redevelopment support`: evaluate TIF, redevelopment support, or property-tax abatement only after parcel and local-authority review.
- `Indiana C-PACE monitor`: track only. Do not underwrite as available funding until a current Indiana path is verified for the deal.
- `Duke Energy Indiana` or `AES Indiana` utility incentives: territory must be confirmed before any rebate assumptions are made.

### Indiana tax considerations to confirm

- Hotel-tax and property-tax assumptions are already embedded in the pro forma and should be cross-checked against current Indiana and local ordinance treatment.
- Any abatement or redevelopment value must be tied to a specific parcel, district, and local approval record.
- Utility rebates, tax credits, and grants remain `estimated` until approved, awarded, and monetizable.

### ESG buildout priorities

- Complete energy model and commissioning path for the hotel base building and rooftop F&B load.
- Confirm water strategy across guestrooms, laundry, kitchen, and irrigation demand.
- Build indoor-air-quality evidence around filtration, ventilation, and low-VOC materials.
- Document local workforce impact for Carmel / Hamilton County hiring and training.
- Preserve source evidence for every claim: energy model, utility pre-approval, tax memo, training plan, and any certification pathway.
